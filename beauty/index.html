Beauty&Beauty

One-page website for a small beauty salon that offers depilation and endermology. It's mainly directed at women, but anybody can use their services.

See the website here.

Summary

The project was focused around two biggest pain points of the old design: information architecture (IA) and UI design. The IA needed a revamp to reflect that the salon is focused on two procedures. The visual side had to change to give an inviting, refreshing but also elegant impression.

After going through all the information on the previous website I proposed an IA and three different visual concepts – the client chose the one they preferred. I designed the website according to the concept and later wrote the website in vanilla HTML, CSS and JS.

Design problem

The biggest problems for the previous website were clarity, visual integrity and not being mobile-friendly. I would include a picture, but I'm not mean enough.

The layout and information architecture were convoluted with unclear names, duplicated information, seemingly random promotional images and no visual hints towards the service the website is supposed to promote. Moreover, the website did not have a mobile version.

I talked with the client about the problems they saw with their website. The main issue was that the website needed a general update, so I focused heavily on the visual side of the project.

Information architecture

The previous website consisted of:

  • landing page with some prices and contact info,
  • 'About us' page,
  • page on depilation with prices,
  • prices page,
  • contact info page.

So the pricing table was repeated 3 times in different configurations, one procedure got much more attention than the other and there were three different places for contact info and text about the salon.

I must admit that the one-pager idea came to me immediately and I just stuck with it, but I still believe there wasn't enough important info to merit a subpage. For tables and longer text I went for expandable titles. I did that first because of scrollability and second because I expect some people will want to read the descriptions, the FAQs and precautions, while others will be good with a short sentence on what, when, where and for how much.

When it comes to information itself, I decided to go with a segmented price table for both procedures and then two separate sections with the same stucture:

  • quick description,
  • icons presenting the time one visit takes, the frequency for the best result,
  • FAQ,
  • contraindications and precautions,
  • more info about the procedure.

Visual concepts

The website needed to give a general impression related to cosmetics and the beauty industry, but this could be taken into different directions. The first one for me was "spa". The website would be relaxing the same way the beauty salon is (anyone who experienced laser depilation will realise why this idea didn't take off). The second idea was heavily influenced by my talks with the client – they showed me other websites they liked, I took elements that could be promising and made something new. The third direction was focused around the effects of the procedures: clean and vivid skin, feeling of freshness and youthfullness. That's why I went with a picture of some perfectly fine legs.

We talked about the designs and chose the third direction, but I still wanted to incorporate the visual elements that the client liked. We also talked about the various menu options – we settled on the third option.

Most important decisions

There were some decisions I should mention here. First is the logo. I decided against doing anything with it. The logo was there and it was already on the sign above the salon's doors and on marketing material. Changing it just to change it or fit the font used on the website would only cause incosistency or (if we were to change all the materials) a huge financial burden.

The second decision was the menu: personally I felt that a side menu fit the style the client showed me, but also gave an opportunity for the logo to be closer to the center of the action (it's a small venue in an increasingly competitive industry, so brand recognition is important). My only regret is not implementing an indicator for the current section. This would increase the feeling of responsiveness, but I did not have the time to learn JS enough.

The third decision was to not include any interactive map on the website. This decision was easy for me, but the opposite's become the standard, so I will explain myself. Firstly, I would not do it well by myself and didn't want to risk it. Secondly, such plugins often break the style of the website. Thirdly, the salon is in a smaller city, so the streets are rather known to people who live there (although I admit some outsider might be left out). The last reason is privacy: the obvious choice would be Google, but those guys aren't really known for respecting data, so I wasn't going to sell the users' data only for the map.

Final design

After the visual concepts and taking some decisions, I designed the rest of the website. I had to edit the picture to give the purple a more mild look, make some icons (and take some from Font Awesome).
